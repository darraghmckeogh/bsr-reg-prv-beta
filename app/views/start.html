Use this service to apply to register a high-rise building.

If you are (or are authorised to act for) the principal accountable person for a building, you can use this service to:

  • check if you need to register the building
  • apply to the register the building
  • continue an application you started previously
  • give us structural information about the building before BSR has approved the buildings registration

Before you start

Once you've started you'll have until 30 September 2023 to complete your application and give us all the inforamtion we need to know about the building.

You'll be asked for the number of floors above ground level. You'll also be asked for the height of the building above ground level in metres.

Help with the floors to count

You'll need to count all floors from ground level to the top of the building. Include:

  • the floor at ground level
  • mezzanine floors that are at least half the largest floor area you have counted in the rest of the building
  • the roof - unless it is only used for machinery or as a plant area

Do not count floors that are:

  • below ground level
  • only used for machinery or plant rooms
Help with ground level

Ground level is the level of land immediately next to the building. If that land is uneven, it is the lowest part of the land immediately next to the building.

Any ceiling below that level is below ground level.

You'll also need to count the bumber of residential units and whether any of these are occupied.

When we have checked that you need to regsiter this building, we will ask you for more information about the building and the dutyholders.

You can use this service as many times as you need. But you can only give us information about 1 building at a time.

This service costs £--- per building. You can pay by credit or debit card.
