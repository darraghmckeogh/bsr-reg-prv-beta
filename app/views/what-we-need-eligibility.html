What we need to know

In this section of your application, we need some information about the building you're registering.

This will determine if your building is in scope. This information will also be stored with your application so it is important that the information you provide is accurate.

You may find it easier to gather this before your start.

We will ask you about:

  • the number of floors from ground level up
  • the height of the building
  • the number of residential units
  • if these residential units are occupied
Help with the floors to count

You'll need to count all floors from ground level to the top of the building. Include:

  • the floor at ground level
  • mezzanine floors that are at least half the largest floor area you have counted in the rest of the building
  • the roof - unless it is only used for machinery or as a plant area

Do not count floors that are:

  • below ground level
  • only used for machinery or plant rooms
Help with ground level

Ground level is the level of land immediately next to the building. If that land is uneven, it is the lowest part of the land immediately next to the building.

Any ceiling below that level is below ground level.

Help with the number of residential units

A residential unit is any place where someone could live, including:

  • a flat
  • a house
  • a maisonette
  • a student bedroom
  • a room or flat in a house of multiple occupation
